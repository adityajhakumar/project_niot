# **SLIDE 6: NOISE POLLUTION & FISH HEARING DAMAGE**

## How Diesel Engines Destroy Marine Communication

### Noise Levels:
| Source | Decibel Level | Impact |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Ambient Ocean (calm)** | 120-130 dB re 1¬µPa | Fish baseline communication |
| **Motorboat/Diesel Engine** | 132-138 dB re 1¬µPa | **+8-10 dB above ambient** |
| **Masking Effect** | 35-40 dB above ambient | Drowns out fish communication at 100-5000 Hz |

### Research Evidence:

**Study: Picciulin et al. (Marine Protected Area - Miramare, Italy)**
- 3 fish species tested (drum, damselfish, goby)
- Boat noise: 132 dB (consistent with diesel engines)
- **Finding:** Hearing thresholds increased by **up to 35 dB** during boat noise exposure
- **Consequence:** Fish cannot detect predators, prey, or mates

**Study: Smith et al. (Goldfish Hearing Loss)**
- 10 minutes motorboat noise exposure ‚Üí **Hearing loss of 10 dB**
- 24 hours continuous exposure ‚Üí **Cumulative hearing loss up to 50%**
- Recovery: Incomplete even after noise stopped

**Behavioral Impact:**
- Disoriented fish = **cannot find food**
- Cannot hear predator alarm signals = **increased predation**
- Cannot locate mates = **reproductive failure**
- Fish avoid noisy fishing grounds = **local stock depletion**

### The Vicious Cycle:
```
Louder diesel engines ‚Üí Fish move away ‚Üí Lower catch ‚Üí 
Fishermen go farther offshore ‚Üí More fuel burned ‚Üí 
Higher costs ‚Üí More debt ‚Üí More intense fishing pressure ‚Üí
```

### Regenerative Charging System:

**How It Works:**
- Propeller spins during boat motion (water flow) or transit
- BLDC motor operates in reverse (generator mode)
- Electricity charges secondary battery while boat moves
- Recovery rate: 13-15% of consumed energy

- At 6.5 knots: 200-250 W regeneration

# **SLIDE 26: RISK MITIGATION & CONTINGENCY PLANNING**

## Addressing Key Concerns

### Risk 1: Battery Theft/Security
**Concern:** High-value battery packs at risk
**Solution:** 
- Batteries taken ashore daily (like fuel cans)
- Secured in fisherman's home overnight
- GPS tracking on each pack
- Insurance program through maritime schemes
**Mitigation Level:** High

### Risk 2: Charging Infrastructure Gaps
**Concern:** Rural villages may lack adequate electricity
**Solution:**
- Harbor charging stations with 50 kW solar arrays
- Grid backup for reliability
- Battery swap model eliminates on-boat charging needs
- Community-based charging stations (government supported)
**Mitigation Level:** High

### Risk 3: Fishermen Technical Knowledge
**Concern:** Limited technical training in fishing communities
**Solution:**
- Modular design requires NO advanced technical knowledge
- Standard 3-control interface (like traditional outboards)
- Training program: 3-day certification
- Mobile technical support team
- Video tutorials in regional languages
**Mitigation Level:** High

### Risk 4: Battery Lifecycle & Disposal
**Concern:** Environmental impact of used batteries
**Solution:**
- LiFePO4 chemistry: 90%+ recyclable
- Battery take-back program (manufacturer responsibility)
- Recycling facility in partnership with e-waste companies
- 2nd life: Use in stationary solar storage systems
- Target: Zero waste to landfill
**Mitigation Level:** High

### Risk 5: Supply Chain Disruption
**Concern:** Motor/battery supply interruptions
**Solution:**
- Dual-vendor strategy (Chinese + Indian manufacturers)
- Safety stock maintained at regional hubs
- Long-term supply contracts with suppliers
- Technology transfer to Indian manufacturers
**Mitigation Level:** Medium-High

### Risk 6: Adoption Resistance
**Concern:** Fishermen loyalty to diesel (known technology)
**Solution:**
- Pilot program with early adopters (50 free units)
- Peer influence: Success stories from neighbors
- 3-year warranty with free replacement guarantee
- Financing through government schemes (low EMI)
- Community demonstrations in local languages
**Mitigation Level:** High
